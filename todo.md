   This is what the client sees immediately. It must answer: "Who is this and are they available?"

Avatar: High-res image.

Display Name: Full name or Username.

Professional Headline: e.g., "Senior Backend Engineer (Node.js/Go)" or "React Native Specialist".

Location & Timezone: e.g., "Berlin, Germany (UTC+1)". Clients need to know overlap hours.

### 2. The "About" Section (The Pitch)
   Bio/Summary: A markdown-supported text block where they describe their passion, soft skills, and working style.

Years of Experience: Calculated automatically from their first job or manually set (e.g., "7 Years").

Languages Spoken: e.g., "English (C2)", "Spanish (Native)".

Video Introduction (Optional but Trending): A 1-2 minute Loom/YouTube embed where they introduce themselves. This builds massive trust.

### 3. The "Tech Stack" (The Filter)
   Don't just use a text block here. Store these as Tags or Relations so clients can filter by them (e.g., "Find me a Geek who knows NestJS").

Primary Skills (Expert): Top 3-5 skills (e.g., React, Node.js, AWS).

Secondary Skills (Competent): (e.g., Docker, Postgres, Redis).

Tools & Methodologies: (e.g., Jira, Scrum, CI/CD, Figma).

### 4. Portfolio / Projects (The Proof)
   This is often more important than the CV for developers.

Role: e.g., "Lead Frontend Developer".

Thumbnail: A screenshot of the app.

Description: "Built a dashboard handling 10k users..."
